What happened when you grew up?

[LegacyKeeper Albert]
Well, in the 1940s, the war started. I was recruited to work at the Lawrence Radiation Lab which required immense patience and adherence to strict engineering protocols. I had to learn how things worked quickly.

Later, in the 1950s, I joined P&G. They were just beginning to implement their Theory Y management styles. I felt like I was acting in this new corporate world, trying to challenge the old guard. 

Then, when I went to Frito-Lay, it was challenging. There were a lot of traditionalists but I found my place by applying rigorous mechanical engineering skills. That printing business I eventually founded allowed me to make between $150,000 and $400,000 a year, securing a great life for my family.
